Frequently Asked Questions

What Is a DIY Approach to Unclogging a Sink?

If your kitchen sink drains slowly, use a plunger to increase pressure by pushing down firmly and then pulling up rapidly to create suction. This helps remove blockages within the drain. If it’s a double-basin sink, make sure to block the unclogged side while plunging the clogged one to maintain proper pressure.

When Should I Call a Plumber for a Clogged Drain?

You should call a plumber when you’ve tried simple DIY fixes like plunging or using hot water and the problem keeps coming back. If you notice water draining slowly, unpleasant odors, or gurgling sounds coming from your drain, it’s a sign there’s a deeper blockage that needs professional attention. At Des Moines, WA Drain Cleaning, our plumbers are trained to find the root cause and get your drains flowing smoothly again.

Can I use chemicals to clean my drain?
