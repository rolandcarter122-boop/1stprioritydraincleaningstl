Frequently Asked Questions

What Is a DIY Approach to Unclogging a Sink?

If your kitchen sink drains slowly, use a plunger to increase pressure by pushing down firmly and then pulling up rapidly to create suction. This helps remove blockages within the drain. If it’s a double-basin sink, make sure to block the unclogged side while plunging the clogged one to maintain proper pressure.

When Should I Call a Plumber for a Clogged Drain?

You should call a plumber when you’ve tried simple DIY fixes like plunging or using hot water and the problem keeps coming back. If you notice water draining slowly, unpleasant odors, or gurgling sounds coming from your drain, it’s a sign there’s a deeper blockage that needs professional attention. At #State Drain Cleaning, our plumbers are trained to find the root cause and get your drains flowing smoothly again.

Do you offer emergency drain cleaning services?

Yes, we do. #State Drain Cleaning provides 24/7 emergency drain cleaning and repair services. Our local plumbers are always ready to respond quickly and fix your drain.

Can I use chemicals to clean my drain?

It’s not ideal, as chemical cleaners tend to corrode your drain pipes if used often or over time. In addition, they don’t completely clear the buildup. Instead, it’s best to find a professional plumber in #State, and you can easily find one with us at #State Drain Cleaning.

Our team in #State provides routine drain maintenance for large-scale commercial properties, including offices, restaurants, factories, and other facilities. This involves our technician scheduling inspection dates, cleaning, and preventive maintenance to keep your entire drainage system running efficiently and prevent costly blockages or breakdowns
